What is our motivation?


Scientific researchers spend a lot of time to read literatures coming out in the field. Keeping up with the relevant papers is very important for designing experiments and writing grant proposals. As the largest collection of biomedical literature, PubMed, comprises more than 26 million publications from life science journals and books. Although hundreds of millions of researchers depend heavily on Pubmed, its search engine remains very basic (mostly key words query). Thus, it is still very time consuming for the users to filter those important articles that are truly relevant.


What is PIPA?


Here we present PIPA - PubMed Intelligent Personalized Assistant, a web-based literature recommendation system aiming to automate biomedical literature search of the PubMed database.

Every day, PIPA records reading history of individual user, crawls PubMed for recently published articles and picks the most relevant ones for each user. PIPA is devoted to help to solve the problem of information overload in higher education and biomedical research.
